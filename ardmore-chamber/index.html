The Starting Point
The Chamber is working.
It just isn't visible enough
to the people it's working for.
You have ribbon cuttings. Networking events. A newsletter. A Facebook page with 6,000 followers. Resources most small businesses would actually find useful — if they knew about them. The problem isn't what you offer. It's that members don't feel the value accumulating between renewals.

What We Heard
A few patterns that
came up in conversation.
  • New members join, attend a ribbon cutting, and then go quiet — because nobody follows up.
  • Members who don't renew probably didn't feel like anything happened for them. We just don't know because there's no exit survey.
  • The current membership packet is dense and wordy. Busy business owners don't read it.
  • The value is real — networking, promotion, events, visibility — but it's not documented anywhere the member can see.
  • Small businesses (the ones most likely to churn) are also the ones least likely to seek out their benefits proactively.

Initiative 02
The first 90 days
set the tone for everything.
Most members who churn probably made the decision to churn within the first 90 days — they just didn't act on it until renewal. A structured onboarding sequence fixes this by creating regular moments of contact and value delivery right when the relationship is being formed.
Day 1
Welcome & What's Next
Personal welcome email, intro to benefits, ribbon cutting scheduling prompt.
Day 14
First Value Touch
Member spotlight questionnaire + invite to next Business After Hours.
Day 45
Engagement Check-In
Upcoming events, Facebook page reminder, Chambermaster setup prompt.
Day 90
First Quarter Review
Summary of Chamber activity, upcoming opportunities, personal note from Kelly.

Initiative 03 — Flagship
Member Impact Decks,
powered by TownSpark.
This is where the proposal gets interesting. TownSpark is a platform we've built — originally for nonprofits — that generates story-driven digital profiles from a business's existing web presence. We're adapting it specifically for chambers of commerce, and Ardmore will be its first pilot.
How it works
Enter a URL. Get a shareable story.
We enter a member's website URL. TownSpark scrapes the site, runs it through our AI generation layer, and produces a polished, story-driven profile page — structured around who the business serves, what they offer, why customers choose them, and how to reach them. The Chamber can share it. The business can share it. And we can track how many people viewed it.

TownSpark — What a Member Gets
A shareable page that
tells their real story.
Each Impact Deck includes
  • Business overview & positioning
  • Who they serve in the community
  • Core services & what makes them different
  • Trust signals (years in business, specialties)
  • Social proof section (if available)
  • "Proud Member of the Ardmore Chamber" badge
  • Contact, website, and CTA links
What the Chamber can do with it
  • Feature in monthly newsletter
  • Share on Facebook as a member spotlight
  • Use in new member recruitment ("look what you get")
  • Link from the Chamber website directory
  • Include in the annual Member Visibility Report
Pilot note
Ardmore is TownSpark's founding pilot client. You'll get this at a founder rate — and when it works, it becomes a case study for chambers nationwide. You're not paying for a prototype. You're getting a proven platform adapted for your context.

Initiative 04 + 05
Renewals that feel like
a thank you, not an invoice.
Retention Strategy
  • Renewal reminder sequence (60-day, 30-day, 7-day)
  • Pre-renewal "here's what's coming" email
  • Anniversary celebration posts (1, 5, 10, 20 years)
  • Exit survey for non-renewers
  • Win-back sequence for lapsed members
Annual Visibility Reports
  • Sent to each member once per year, ahead of renewal
  • Documents every Chamber mention, share, and spotlight
  • Includes estimated audience reach per activity
  • TownSpark page views and CTA clicks
  • Generated from Jordan's simple activity log — minimal overhead
